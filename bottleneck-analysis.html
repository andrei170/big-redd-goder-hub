Lead Doc

Big Redd / GoDer Stack | Bottleneck Analysis

Prepared: 2026-05-16 Audience tier: 82K IG (mid-range, 30K-150K). Per-bottleneck Y1 floor: $30K-$120K. Combined ceiling: $150K-$500K.

Bottleneck #1 - 82K IG audience routes to a redirect, not a website

Snapshot. godermag.com 301-redirects straight to the @godermagazine IG profile. There is no website. There is no email capture, no offer page, no event page, no shop, no member area. 82K followers on the personal account plus the @godermagazine following plus the five other entity accounts all funnel into a closed Instagram loop. The 18-year magazine brand has zero owned digital surface outside Meta's platform.

Asymmetry. A 30K-150K audience tier on a B2B-leaning operator (event + magazine + RE + radio) should be running an owned hub that captures email, monetizes ads, sells brand-feature placements, and lists upcoming events. The current state is the opposite: the platform owns the audience and Big Redd is one IG algorithm tweak away from losing it.

Cost of waiting. Every week without an owned hub is a week of unconverted audience. At the 30K-150K tier, the floor on a working magazine hub (ad placements + brand-feature waitlist + email list) is $30K-$120K Year-1.

Fix. GoDer Magazine hub at godermag.com (real site, not redirect): featured-issue grid, brand-feature application form, advertiser pack, email opt-in, calendar of GoDer events / expos / Yukmouth Network plays. Ships in 7 days.

Year-1 impact floor. $40K-$120K direct (advertiser placements + feature applications) + email list compounds for life.

Why this is #1. It is the load-bearing fix. Everything else in this stack assumes there is a website. Right now there isn't one.

Bottleneck #2 - World CBD Expo domain is parked

Snapshot. worldcbdexpo.com returns a 114-byte HTML shell that JS-redirects to a GoDaddy parking lander. No event page. No dates. No ticket flow. No speaker list. No sponsor pack. No contact form. Verified by fetch on 2026-05-16.

Asymmetry. A paid trade-show event with the network you have around it (Yukmouth, urban entertainment, CBD industry) should be selling booths and tickets through a real funnel. A parked domain on a "World CBD Expo" search query reads to advertisers and exhibitors as "this event is dormant." That kills booth-sales conversations before they start.

Cost of waiting. Each event cycle the booking funnel does not exist is one event cycle of booth + ticket revenue not collected. CBD expos at the mid-tier scale typically transact $2K-$15K per booth, 30-80 booths per event. The web-surface gap is gating that entire revenue line.

Fix. Single-page expo hub at worldcbdexpo.com: dates / venue / agenda, exhibitor application, sponsor pack download (gated email), attendee ticket flow, speaker roster, returning-exhibitor wall. Stripe-integrated booth checkout. Ships in 7 days from kickoff (or hedged: if the expo is paused on purpose, ignore - if not, this is the first fix).

Year-1 impact floor. $30K-$120K incremental ticket + booth revenue, with the heavier lift coming from speaker / sponsor sequence in year two.

Why this is #2. It is the most embarrassing public-facing gap. A parked domain on a paid event is a credibility bleed every time a potential advertiser or speaker searches for the expo.

Bottleneck #3 - 82K IG audience and zero email list

Snapshot. No newsletter. No magazine subscriber list. No expo waitlist. No "get the new issue" email. The 82K IG following is not converting to owned subscribers anywhere across the six-entity stack.

Asymmetry. GoDer Magazine is an 18-year publishing brand. Publishing brands monetize through subscriber lists. The economics of magazine ad placements, brand features, and event ticket sales all run on email. The personal Big Redd brand and the magazine brand both have audiences and neither has a list.

Cost of waiting. Every IG post that lands on a follower who is not yet on a list is a missed list-build event. A magazine-style list at the 30K-150K tier converts at 3-7% from IG over 6 months: that is 2,500-5,700 incremental subscribers. Each subscriber is worth $1-$3 per month on a culture-magazine ad-and-event model. The list does not exist, so neither does that revenue.

Fix. "The GoDer Drop" - a weekly culture digest (new music, fashion picks, expo dates, network features). Opt-in lives on the new godermag.com hub, the new worldcbdexpo.com hub, and the IG bio link. Segmented routes: music fans / brand advertisers / expo attendees / RE deal flow.

Year-1 impact floor. $30K-$80K from ad placements + event ticket sales attributable to email + featured-brand applications. Compounds for life of the list.

Why this is #3. Mid-leverage. It compounds, but only after #1 and #2 exist as landing destinations.

Bottleneck #4 - No info-product / community / mastermind on top of the network

Snapshot. Big Redd is positioned as 10X-KING / BOSS / CEO across IG. The network is real (Yukmouth, E-40 / Lil Wayne / Keak Da Sneak proximity, GoDer Magazine 18-year history, multi-entity operator). The audience is leaning in. There is no productized offer on top of any of that. No "how I built six businesses" course. No founder community. No paid mastermind. No coaching ladder.

Asymmetry. A 10X-positioned operator with 82K targeted followers and a documented multi-entity stack has the strongest possible permission to sell an info-product or a community on top of the existing brand. The permission is there. The product is not.

Cost of waiting. Every quarter without a productized layer is a quarter of high-margin revenue not captured. A founders-style community at $50-$200/month, even at 200-500 members, is $120K-$1.2M Year-1.

Fix. "The GODER Network" - private community for operators who want into the Bay Area / West Coast urban entertainment, CBD, and RE network. $97/month founder rate, capped at 250 founding members. Monthly Q&A with Big Redd. Bi-weekly drop-in calls with rotating Yukmouth Network guests. Marketplace board for advertiser / sponsor / RE deal flow.

Year-1 impact floor. $60K-$150K direct community revenue, with the community itself becoming the warm-pipeline for premium expo booths and magazine features.

Why this is #4. High ceiling, longer time-to-revenue. Worth surfacing because it unlocks the highest-margin line in the whole stack once #1 and #2 exist.

Bottleneck #5 - Cross-business handoff is not designed

Snapshot. Six entities (GoDer Magazine, World CBD Expo, iHeartRealEstate, Smoke A Lot Radio, Yukmouth Network, Snorkle Snoodle USA). Zero observable cross-sell. Magazine readers are not routed to expo tickets. Expo attendees are not routed to RE deal flow. Radio listeners are not routed to magazine subscriptions. Each entity is a silo.

Asymmetry. A multi-entity operator with overlapping audience segments leaves money on every cross-sell that does not exist. A magazine reader interested in a music feature is also an above-average candidate to attend the CBD expo, listen to Smoke A Lot Radio, and engage with Yukmouth Network content. Zero of that traffic is captured.

Cost of waiting. Each entity is paying full acquisition cost (in attention, content, time) for its own audience. The combined network effect is uncaptured.

Fix. A single GoDer-stack router. Bio-link on @networkgoder, footer link on all six entity surfaces, and the email sequence: "Which side of the network are you here for?" routes to one of four follow-up tracks (Magazine reader / Brand advertiser / Expo attendee / RE deal-flow). One source of truth, six entry doors.

Year-1 impact floor. $20K-$60K incremental from net-new cross-sells (low end - this one compounds harder in year two).

Why this is #5. Lower-priority install, higher-leverage compound. Worth scoping at kickoff, installable in week 2 once the hubs exist.

Why these five

The default operator with 82K followers has one bottleneck (no funnel). Big Redd has five because he runs six entities and none of them have a live landing surface. The fix is not "build a funnel" - the fix is "build six entities one website each, then plumb them together." Phase 1 (hubs #1 + #2) ships in 7 days. Phase 2 (#3 + #5) ships in week 2. Phase 3 (#4) is post-install scaling.
