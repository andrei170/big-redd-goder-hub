THE FIVE BOTTLENECKS

Where the GoDer Stack
Is Losing Revenue Right Now

Five bottlenecks across six entities. Each one is a real cap on revenue that could be collected today. Combined Year-1 floor if all five are fixed: $180K to $530K.

01

82K IG audience routes to a redirect, not a website

godermag.com 301-redirects to @godermagazine IG. No website. No email capture. No offer page. No event page. 18 years of GoDer Magazine and the brand's only owned digital surface is a social profile Meta can de-rank or ban at any time.

Cost of Waiting

Every week without an owned hub is a week of unconverted audience. At this tier, the floor on a working magazine hub is $30K-$120K Year-1 from ad placements and feature applications alone.

Fix: GoDer Magazine hub at godermag.com. Featured-issue grid, brand-feature application form, advertiser pack, email opt-in, event calendar. Ships in 7 days.
Year-1 impact floor: $40K-$120K
02

World CBD Expo domain is parked

worldcbdexpo.com returns a GoDaddy parking lander. No event page. No dates. No ticket flow. No speaker list. No sponsor pack. No contact form. Every advertiser, exhibitor, and attendee who Googles the event name lands on a dead page.

Cost of Waiting

A parked domain on a paid event reads as "dormant" to every potential exhibitor. That kills booth-sales conversations before they start. CBD expos at this tier typically transact $2K-$15K per booth, 30-80 booths per event.

Fix: Single-page expo hub at worldcbdexpo.com. Dates, venue, exhibitor application, sponsor pack (email-gated), Stripe-integrated booth checkout, speaker roster. Ships in 7 days. (If the expo is paused - this hub ships in waitlist mode.)
Year-1 impact floor: $30K-$120K
03

82K IG audience and zero email list

No newsletter. No magazine subscriber list. No expo waitlist. No "get the new issue" email. The 82K IG following is not converting to any owned subscriber asset across all six entities.

Cost of Waiting

A magazine-style list at this tier converts at 3-7% from IG over 6 months: 2,500-5,700 incremental subscribers from what already exists. Each worth $1-$3/month on a culture-magazine model. The list does not exist, so neither does that revenue.

Fix: "The GoDer Drop" - weekly culture digest. Opt-in on both hubs, bio link, all six entity accounts. Four segments: magazine readers, brand advertisers, expo attendees, RE deal flow.
Year-1 impact floor: $30K-$80K
04

No community or info-product on top of the network

Big Redd is positioned as 10X-KING / BOSS / CEO. The network is real. Yukmouth, E-40 proximity, GoDer Magazine 18-year history, multi-entity operator. The audience is leaning in. There is no productized offer on top of any of it.

Cost of Waiting

A founders-style community at $97/month, 250 founding members is $291,000/year direct. The permission for this exists. The product does not.

Fix (Phase 3): "The GODER Network" - private community for operators in the Bay Area / West Coast urban entertainment, CBD, and RE space. Monthly Q&A, bi-weekly drop-in calls with Yukmouth Network guests, marketplace board.
Year-1 impact floor: $60K-$150K
05

Cross-business handoff is not designed

Six entities. Zero observable cross-sell. Magazine readers do not route to expo tickets. Expo attendees do not route to RE deal flow. Radio listeners do not route to magazine subscriptions. Each entity is a silo.

Cost of Waiting

Each entity pays full acquisition cost in attention and content for its own audience. The combined network effect of six overlapping audience segments is completely uncaptured.

Fix: One GoDer-stack router. Bio link on @networkgoder, footer link on all six entity surfaces. Email sequence routes: "Which side of the network are you here for?" Four follow-up tracks.
Year-1 impact floor: $20K-$60K
