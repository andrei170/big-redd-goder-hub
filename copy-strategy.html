Big Redd | Copy Strategy

Prepared: 2026-05-16

Voice register

Match. Urban / hip-hop / 10X-mindset / operator-direct / West Coast cadence.

Tokens to mirror (from his own surfaces). - "10X", "KING", "BOSS", "CEO" - "GODER" / "GoDer" - tribe codeword - Short bars, punchlines, status-led identity language - No corporate softness, no methodology-led elegance, no soft-feminine register

Tokens to avoid. - "synergy", "ecosystem", "bandwidth", "stakeholder", "holistic" - "journey", "transformation" (as filler) - "leverage" (as a verb) - Any phrase from the banned-AI list

Cadence. Operator-direct. Numbers first. Receipts second. Hype third. Never the other way around.

Primary audience

Big Redd's audience splits four ways: 1. Brand advertisers looking for placement in GoDer Magazine + cultural credibility 2. CBD industry operators looking for booth + sponsor placement at World CBD Expo 3. Real-estate investors / agents plugging into iHeartRealEstate deal flow 4. Urban music + culture fans following the Yukmouth / Smoke A Lot Radio network

Each segment is an entry door into the same six-entity network. The new GoDer hub plus the email list segments the four routes from one URL.

Core pain points (read from the public surfaces)

  • Built six businesses, no website on five of them. The brand exists in audiences' heads, not on the internet. Search "World CBD Expo" - what comes back is a parked GoDaddy lander.
  • 18-year magazine brand running through IG only. GoDer Magazine since 2007. Zero owned digital surface. Every advertiser conversation starts with "do you have a media kit?" and the answer is currently no.
  • 82K IG audience, zero email list. The platform owns the relationship. One algorithm change kills the network.
  • Multi-entity operator, zero cross-sell. Magazine readers do not flow to expo tickets. Expo attendees do not flow to RE deal flow. Each entity is a silo.

Desire state

What the prospect wants to become: - The Bay Area / West Coast urban operator whose six entities ALL show up on a Google search. Not just the IG handle. - The publisher whose brand-feature application is gated, waitlisted, and pulls advertisers without DMing. - The event founder whose expo sells booths through a real funnel, not "DM me for info." - The network operator whose audience can be reached without IG's permission.

Objection handling

Objection Counter
"I'm too busy with the magazine and the network to build a site right now" Install ships in 7 days. You don't build, my team does. You approve copy and color, sign off on the live URLs.
"I tried to build this before and it stalled" Most agencies fragment work across 5 specialists who don't talk. One operator, one point of contact, faster execution.
"What if I move on the call this time and it doesn't pan out" The hub is yours whether we work together or not. The plan is built, the receipts are in the package.
"Why six websites instead of one" Because you run six businesses. The brand stack only compounds if each entity has a real surface. Otherwise advertisers, exhibitors, and RE leads bounce.

Campaign angles (3)

Angle A - "18-year brand, no website"

Headline: GoDer Magazine has been running since 2007. The website is a redirect. Hook: 18 years of culture coverage. 82K following. Zero owned digital surface. The brand exists. The website doesn't. Fix it in 7 days.

Angle B - "The Expo without an Expo Page"

Headline: World CBD Expo. Domain parked. Hook: Search the event name. What comes back is a GoDaddy lander. Every advertiser, exhibitor, and attendee who Googles the event gets that. The first 7-day fix is a real event hub with booth checkout, sponsor pack, and dates.

Angle C - "Six businesses, one router"

Headline: The GoDer Stack is six entities. None of them cross-sell. Hook: Magazine readers should flow to expo tickets. Expo attendees should flow to RE deal flow. Right now they don't. One router, six entry doors, one email list.

What NOT to write

  • No "elevate your brand" / "unlock your potential" / "transformative" filler
  • No "in today's fast-paced world" intros
  • No "synergy" / "ecosystem" corporate softness
  • No "for ambitious entrepreneurs" generic identity copy - Big Redd is the operator, name him
  • No fabricated testimonials. Receipts block stays empty with a placeholder until kickoff.
  • No fabricated legal name. Use "Big Redd" or "@networkgoder".
